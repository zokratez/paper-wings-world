# Master Paper Airplane Library (35+ Curated Designs)

This is the **single source of truth** for all paper airplane designs considered for Paper Wings World.

Sourced primarily from **FoldNfly.com** (the most comprehensive and well-tested public database with difficulty ratings and performance data), cross-referenced with Red Bull Paper Wings official designs, John Collins' record-holding work, and competition circuits.

**Total curated here**: 36 high-quality, distinct, educationally valuable designs.

Each entry includes:
- **Difficulty** (Easy / Medium / Hard / Expert)
- **Primary Category** (and secondary tags)
- **Short description**
- **Flight characteristics**
- **Cultural / Historical note** (when meaningful)

---

## 1. Classic Beginners & Darts

**Best for teaching, first flights, speed, and simple distance.**

- **Basic Dart**  
  Difficulty: Easy  
  Category: Classic Dart / Distance  
  The iconic 5-fold schoolyard plane. Forgiving of imperfect folds.  
  Flight: Fast, straight, decent distance for its simplicity.  
  Note: The universal first paper airplane most people learn. Ideal starter for children.

- **Lock-Bottom**  
  Difficulty: Easy  
  Category: Dart / Distance + Stability  
  Reinforced base for better rigidity.  
  Flight: Straighter and more stable than basic darts.  
  Note: Popular variation that adds one extra locking fold.

- **The Stable**  
  Difficulty: Easy  
  Category: Classic / Acrobatic  
  Wide, stable profile with good looping potential.  
  Flight: Predictable with nice wide loops when adjusted.  
  Note: Excellent "second plane" after the Basic Dart.

- **Raven**  
  Difficulty: Easy  
  Category: Dart / Distance  
  Sleek, dark, bird-inspired silhouette.  
  Flight: Good speed and straight-line distance.  
  Note: Visually striking for kids.

- **Arrowhead**  
  Difficulty: Easy-Medium  
  Category: Dart / Precision  
  Sharp, pointed design.  
  Flight: Fast and accurate when thrown well.  
  Note: Popular in classrooms for its clean lines.

- **Lift Off**  
  Difficulty: Easy  
  Category: Dart + Launcher Compatible  
  Designed to work with rubber-band catapults.  
  Flight: Excellent distance with launcher assist.  
  Note: Great for demonstrating how extra launch energy changes performance.

## 2. Gliders & Long-Distance Flyers

**Best for graceful flight, hang time, and real distance.**

- **Stealth Glider** (FoldNfly top pick for time aloft)  
  Difficulty: Medium  
  Category: Glider / Time Aloft  
  Wide wing area, designed for slow floating flight.  
  Flight: Exceptional duration (6+ seconds in tests); circles gracefully when tuned.  
  Note: One of the best "real glider" feeling designs on the site.

- **The Bird** (FoldNfly top pick for distance)  
  Difficulty: Hard  
  Category: Distance Glider  
  Bird-like accordion wings for stability and lift.  
  Flight: Outstanding long glides (50+ ft regular throws, much farther with power).  
  Note: Frequently cited as the best overall distance performer in FoldNfly testing.

- **Suzanne** (John Collins / 2012 Guinness record holder)  
  Difficulty: Medium-Hard (precision critical)  
  Category: High-Performance Glider  
  Famous world-record glider with variable dihedral.  
  Flight: True efficient gliding with lift, long straight flights (200+ ft possible).  
  Note: Thrown by Joe Ayoob for the 69.14 m record in 2012. Still one of the most replicated high-performance designs. Named after Collins' wife.

- **Fast Glider**  
  Difficulty: Medium  
  Category: Glider / Speed + Distance  
  Looks more like a real airplane than most paper designs.  
  Flight: Fast glide with good distance and stability.  
  Note: Popular choice when players want something that "looks real."

- **Nakamichi Glider** (and close variants)  
  Difficulty: Medium  
  Category: Long-Distance Glider  
  Japanese design lineage emphasizing wide wings and locked structure.  
  Flight: Excellent long, stable glides.  
  Note: One of the most respected families of real-world paper glider designs.

- **Sea Glider**  
  Difficulty: Medium  
  Category: Glider / Decorative  
  Elegant, wide-winged profile.  
  Flight: Slow, graceful floating.  
  Note: Beautiful on the water or over lakes.

- **Hunting Flight**  
  Difficulty: Medium  
  Category: Distance + Time Aloft  
  Balanced performer.  
  Flight: Strong combination of range and duration.  
  Note: Versatile all-rounder.

## 3. Stunt, Spinner & Acrobatic

**Best for loops, spins, corkscrews, and "wow" moments.**

- **Light Spinner** (FoldNfly top acrobatic pick)  
  Difficulty: Medium  
  Category: Acrobatic / Spinner  
  Creates beautiful corkscrew spirals. Wing angle controls spiral width.  
  Flight: Unique spinning descent that feels like a helicopter or falling leaf.  
  Note: One of the most visually delightful designs for spectators.

- **Loop Plane**  
  Difficulty: Easy-Medium  
  Category: Acrobatic / Loops  
  Specifically engineered for vertical loops.  
  Flight: Can perform clean loop-the-loops with the right throw and trim.  
  Note: A favorite for showing that paper planes can do "real" aerobatics.

- **Tail Spin**  
  Difficulty: Easy  
  Category: Acrobatic / Spin  
  Simple design that induces autorotation.  
  Flight: Spins as it descends.  
  Note: Great early success plane for young builders.

- **Crescent Boomerang** (Red Bull Paper Wings Aerobatics winner influence)  
  Difficulty: Medium-Hard  
  Category: Acrobatic / Return  
  Boomerang-style design used by recent Red Bull champions.  
  Flight: Can be made to return or perform dramatic arcs.  
  Note: Modern competition favorite (e.g., 2022 Red Bull Aerobatics winner).

- **Spin Plane**  
  Difficulty: Hard  
  Category: Acrobatic  
  Advanced spinning design.  
  Flight: Tight, fast spins.  
  Note: Requires precise symmetry.

- **The Sprinter**  
  Difficulty: Medium  
  Category: Acrobatic + Distance  
  Fast with some stunt capability.  
  Flight: Quick with playful behavior.  
  Note: Good bridge between dart and stunt categories.

## 4. Ring, Hoop & Annular

**Unusual but extremely stable and educational flight profiles.**

- **The Ring** (Red Bull Paper Wings official "Hangtime" base design)  
  Difficulty: Medium  
  Category: Annular / Time Aloft  
  Two connected rings forming a tube.  
  Flight: Remarkable stability and slow descent due to vortex effects.  
  Note: One of Red Bull's three official "folding school" starter designs. Feels almost sci-fi.

- **The UFO**  
  Difficulty: Medium  
  Category: Annular / Acrobatic  
  Saucer-like profile.  
  Flight: Floats and wobbles in interesting ways.  
  Note: Always a crowd-pleaser with kids.

- **Ring Variants** (multiple modern annular designs)  
  Difficulty: Medium  
  Category: Annular / Stability  
  Various refinements on the ring concept.  
  Flight: Exceptional forgiveness and calm flight.  
  Note: Excellent for demonstrating how non-traditional shapes can outperform "normal" planes.

## 5. Canard, Delta & Advanced Wing

**Forward wings, deltas, and more sophisticated aerodynamics.**

- **Canard** (FoldNfly)  
  Difficulty: Hard  
  Category: Canard / Advanced  
  Small forward wings ahead of the main wing.  
  Flight: Surprisingly stable and efficient once trimmed; counter-intuitive look.  
  Note: Real aircraft (and some birds) use canards. Great teaching opportunity.

- **Sonic Jet** (FoldNfly fastest)  
  Difficulty: Hard  
  Category: Delta / Speed  
  Sleek, high-speed profile.  
  Flight: Very fast (nearly 20 mph in tests). Requires tight construction.  
  Flight: Cuts through air cleanly.  
  Note: One of the most "jet-like" paper designs.

- **Delta Wing** (modern sport style — our v1 plane)  
  Difficulty: Medium  
  Category: Delta / Speed + Agility  
  Large triangular wings.  
  Flight: Fast, agile, loves powerful throws.  
  Note: Used on real supersonic aircraft. Looks dramatic in flight.

- **V-Wing**  
  Difficulty: Medium  
  Category: Advanced Wing  
  Distinct V-shaped wing platform.  
  Flight: Good stability and interesting glide.  
  Note: Visually unique.

- **Zip Dart**  
  Difficulty: Hard  
  Category: Dart / Advanced  
  Refined, high-performance dart.  
  Flight: Excellent speed + distance combination.  
  Note: Popular with serious paper airplane enthusiasts.

## Library System — Built for Easy Expansion

The goal is to make adding planes 2–4 hours of **content work**, not engineering work.

### Recommended Data Architecture (Unity)

**Core Asset Type**: `PaperPlaneDefinition` (ScriptableObject or Addressable JSON + Prefab bundle)

Each plane asset contains:
- Metadata (name, difficulty, primary/secondary categories, cultural note, estimated fold time, unlock tier)
- `FoldingStep[]` array (step number, short text, 3D animation clip or parameter set, validation collider zones, success audio)
- Reference to 3D Paper Prefab (with its own bending rig or blend shapes)
- Flight Tuning Parameters (lift curve, drag, stability, wind sensitivity, recovery behavior)
- Printable Template reference (PDF or generated at runtime)
- Thumbnail / Icon for UI
- Optional: Region affinity tags (for future "this plane flies great over mountains" hints)

**Central Service**: `PaperPlaneLibrary` (singleton or ScriptableObject database)
- Loads all registered planes via Addressables at startup
- Provides queries: `GetFreePlanes()`, `GetAllByDifficulty()`, `GetByCategory()`, `GetPlane(id)`
- Manages unlock state (synced with RevenueCat / local save)

**Folding Screen**
- Completely generic. It receives a `PaperPlaneDefinition` and renders the steps dynamically.
- No hard-coded UI per plane.

**Flight Scene**
- Receives the plane's tuning parameters + 3D prefab.
- Wind/thermal system uses the plane's sensitivity values.
- Recovery and "personality" come from the definition.

**Adding a New Plane (Post-v1.0)**
1. Create new `PaperPlaneDefinition` asset
2. Author the 3D model + folding steps (the hard/creative part)
3. Drop the asset into the Addressables group
4. Register it in the library (or auto-discover)
5. Add to progression / monetization config
6. Done — appears in the library browser, folding screen, and flight selector

This design makes the "full 35+ library" a pure content pipeline after the first 8 are proven.
