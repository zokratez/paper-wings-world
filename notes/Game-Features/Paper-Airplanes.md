## Detailed Profiles

### 1. Classic Dart (Beginner)

**Difficulty**: Beginner  
**Category**: Dart / Speed  
**Short description**: The timeless 5-fold schoolyard dart. The plane almost everyone folds first.

**Flight characteristics**: Fast, direct, satisfying speed. Moderate stability. Best thrown hard and level or slightly upward. Great for straight-line races.

**Cultural/historical note**: The universal starter paper airplane taught to children worldwide for generations.

**Why it's fun or educational**: Instant success on the first or second try. Perfectly teaches the basics of symmetry, sharp leading edges, and how speed affects flight. Ideal first plane in the app.

---

### 2. The Ring (Beginner)

**Difficulty**: Beginner  
**Category**: Ring / Annular / Stability  
**Short description**: Two connected rings forming a tube. One of Red Bull Paper Wings' three official "folding school" designs.

**Flight characteristics**: Extremely stable and slow. Floats gently with almost no tendency to dive or stall. Creates visible vortex effects.

**Cultural/historical note**: Featured in Red Bull's global Paper Wings competition as the official hangtime/stability starter design.

**Why it's fun or educational**: Feels almost magical. Kids are amazed it flies at all. Excellent for teaching how non-traditional shapes can be more stable than "normal" airplanes. One of the most forgiving designs for young builders.

---

### 3. Loop Plane (Beginner)

**Difficulty**: Beginner  
**Category**: Stunt / Acrobatic  
**Short description**: A simple design specifically engineered to perform clean vertical loops.

**Flight characteristics**: With the right throw angle and speed, it will loop-the-loop reliably. Very responsive to small trim adjustments.

**Cultural/historical note**: Modern design created to demonstrate that paper airplanes can do real aerobatics.

**Why it's fun or educational**: Provides immediate, repeatable "wow" moments. Teaches cause-and-effect (throw angle, speed, and elevator trim). Turns folding into play instantly.

---

### 4. Nakamichi Glider (Intermediate)

**Difficulty**: Intermediate  
**Category**: Long Distance Glider  
**Short description**: A refined Japanese glider design emphasizing wide wings, slight dihedral, and a locked rear structure.

**Flight characteristics**: Excellent long, graceful glides. Trades raw speed for distance and stability. Forgiving in light wind.

**Cultural/historical note**: Part of the respected Nakamichi family of Japanese paper airplane designs, known worldwide in the paper aviation community.

**Why it's fun or educational**: Feels like a real sailplane. Players learn the value of wing area and dihedral for lift. Strong cultural tie-in for the "world" theme of the app.

---

### 5. Stealth Glider (Intermediate)

**Difficulty**: Intermediate  
**Category**: Time Aloft Glider  
**Short description**: FoldNfly's top-rated design for maximum time in the air. Wide wing area optimized for slow, floating flight.

**Flight characteristics**: Exceptional duration (often 6+ seconds in testing). Slow, circling glides when properly trimmed. Best launched upward from height.

**Cultural/historical note**: One of the most tested and recommended gliders in the modern paper airplane community (FoldNfly).

**Why it's fun or educational**: Rewards patience and fine-tuning. Perfect plane for the 3D globe — players will want to keep it aloft while exploring canyons and coastlines. Teaches trimming and launch technique.

---

### 6. Light Spinner (Intermediate)

**Difficulty**: Intermediate  
**Category**: Stunt / Spinner  
**Short description**: Creates beautiful, hypnotic corkscrew spirals as it descends. Wing angle controls the tightness of the spin.

**Flight characteristics**: Unique autorotation flight path. Very visually engaging. Can be adjusted for wide lazy spirals or tighter spins.

**Cultural/historical note**: Popularized by FoldNfly as their top acrobatic recommendation.

**Why it's fun or educational**: One of the most delightful planes to watch in flight. Kids (and adults) laugh and experiment with the wing angles. Great for the "flight personality" system in the simulator.

---

### 7. Canard (Advanced)

**Difficulty**: Advanced  
**Category**: Advanced Wing / Educational  
**Short description**: Features small forward wings (canards) ahead of the main wing — a real aerodynamic concept used on many modern aircraft.

**Flight characteristics**: Unusual appearance but excellent, stable glide once correctly folded and trimmed. Counter-intuitive but highly effective.

**Cultural/historical note**: Based on French paper aviation experiments and the canard configuration used in real aviation (Burt Rutan designs, some fighters, and birds).

**Why it's fun or educational**: The strongest teaching plane in the set. Players learn that real airplanes use canards for pitch control. Looks dramatically different in the air and on the folding table.

---

### 8. The Bird (Advanced)

**Difficulty**: Advanced  
**Category**: Long Distance  
**Short description**: FoldNfly's top-rated distance champion. Features distinctive "bird-like" accordion wings for stability and lift.

**Flight characteristics**: Outstanding long glides. Regular throws of 50+ feet; much farther with good technique and power. Very stable when symmetrical.

**Cultural/historical note**: Frequently cited in paper airplane communities as one of the best-performing distance designs ever documented on the site.

**Why it's fun or educational**: The "hero" long-distance plane. Players feel real accomplishment when they master the folds and throw. Perfect capstone for the paid tier and excellent for dramatic low-altitude canyon flights in the simulator.

## Library System Reminder

All 8 planes are implemented as data-driven `PaperPlaneDefinition` assets. Adding planes from the master library later is designed to be content-only work. See the expansion architecture in the previous section of this file.

## Library System — Designed for Easy Expansion

The 8 planes above are only the beginning. The entire experience (folding tutorials, 3D models, flight physics, unlock flow, and UI) is built as a **data-driven library** so that adding planes from the master list in `Research/Paper-Airplane-Library.md` becomes primarily content work.

### Technical Architecture (Recommended)

**PaperPlaneDefinition** (ScriptableObject + Addressable)
- Metadata (name, difficulty, categories, cultural note, estimated time, unlock tier)
- FoldingStep[] (text, 3D animation parameters or clip reference, validation zones, success conditions)
- 3D Paper Prefab reference (with bending rig)
- Flight Tuning (lift/drag curves, stability, wind sensitivity, recovery style)
- Printable template asset
- UI thumbnail + voice line (future)

**PaperPlaneLibrary Service**
- Loads the full catalog via Addressables at startup
- Exposes clean queries: `GetFreePlanes()`, `GetByCategory()`, `GetPlaneById()`
- Owns unlock state (synced with RevenueCat)

**Generic Screens**
- FoldingScreen and FlightSelector are 100% plane-agnostic. They just receive a `PaperPlaneDefinition`.
- Adding a new plane never requires touching UI or folding engine code.

### Adding a New Plane (After v1.0)

1. Author the 3D model and folding steps (the creative work)
2. Create the `PaperPlaneDefinition` asset
3. Drop it into the correct Addressables group
4. Add it to the progression/monetization config
5. It automatically appears everywhere

**Goal**: Adding planes 4, 5, 6... should feel like "dropping in new content," not "new feature development."

See `Research/Paper-Airplane-Library.md` → "Library System — Built for Easy Expansion" for the full recommended schema.
